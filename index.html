Profile

Educator first — surgery, imaging and invention in service of better care.

I'm a Professor in the College of Medicine at National Chung Hsing University, where teaching and mentorship are at the heart of my work. I educate and supervise medical students and residents, serve as an OSCE examiner for Taiwan's medical-education board, and aim to connect the classroom, the laboratory, and the operating room so that the next generation of physicians learns medicine that reaches the bedside.

Alongside that academic role I am a senior orthopaedic surgeon — Deputy Director of the Department of Orthopedics and Chief of Pediatric Orthopaedics at Changhua Christian Hospital. My clinical practice centers on hip-preservation surgery, pediatric orthopaedics, trauma and joint reconstruction, including the early diagnosis and treatment of developmental dysplasia of the hip (DDH).

I hold a Ph.D. in biomedical imaging, and I bring that dual training to translational research and medical-device innovation — reflected in multiple patented orthopaedic devices, several addressing neonatal hip care. Teaching, research, and surgery reinforce one another in everything I do.
